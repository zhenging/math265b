The Divergence Test

Theorem 9.8 Divergence Test

If ∑ak\sum a_k∑ak​ converges, then lim⁡k→∞ak=0\dlim_{k \to \infty} a_k=0k→∞lim​ak​=0. Equivalently, if lim⁡k→∞ak≠0\dlim_{k \to \infty} a_k \ne 0k→∞lim​ak​≠0, then the series diverges.

Important note: Theorem 9.8 can not be used to determine convergence.

Proof

Let {Sn}\{ S_n \}{Sn​} be the sequence of partial sums for the series ∑ak\sum a_k∑ak​ . Assuming the series converges, it has a finite value, call it SSS, where

S=lim⁡k→∞Sk=lim⁡k→∞Sk−1.\begin{aligned} S = \lim_{k \to \infty} S_k = \lim_{k \to \infty} S_{k-1}. \end{aligned} S=k→∞lim​Sk​=k→∞lim​Sk−1​.​

Note that Sk−1−Sk=akS_{k-1} - S_k = a_kSk−1​−Sk​=ak​. Therefore,

lim⁡k→∞ak=lim⁡k→∞(Sk−1−Sk)=S−S=0\begin{aligned} \lim_{k \to \infty} a_k = \lim_{k \to \infty} (S_{k-1} - S_k) = S - S = 0 \end{aligned} k→∞lim​ak​=k→∞lim​(Sk−1​−Sk​)=S−S=0​

that is, lim⁡k→∞ak=0\dlim_{k \to \infty} a_k=0k→∞lim​ak​=0. The second part of the test follows immediately because it is the contrapositive of the first part.
